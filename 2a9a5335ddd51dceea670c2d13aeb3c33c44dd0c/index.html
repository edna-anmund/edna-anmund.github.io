Getting there

Carlshälls Gård can be found on the island of Långholmen in the very centre of Stockholm.

Whilst in theory you can drive to the venue, this is not recommended as Sweden has a zero tolerance policy on driving under the influence. Instead, we recommend that you either arrange a taxi (Uber and Bolt are readily available), or take the metro. The nearest station is Hornstull (accessible via the red and blue lines) which is approximately a fifteen minute walk, much of which is on the island itself.

Accommodation

The Långholmen Hotell is by far the most convenient location to stay, being a mere five minute walk through a cute, adjacent allotment. The hotel itself was originally a prison in the 1800s but is, nevertheless, apparently quite nice.

There are plenty of alternative hotels and AirBnbs in the surrounding area which are either a short walk or a quick taxi ride away. Taxis should be available throughout the evening.

Getting Around

Getting around Stockholm is very straightforward. All the main areas are accessible via tram or bus. The metro is very good, spans much of the city, and can be used with just your bank card. Ubers, Bolts, and taxis are also available but are a little more expensive than elsewhere. Alternatively, the city is very walkable.

What To Do

Sweden's capital is a city of islands, and has lots going on - from art galleries, to the old town (Gamla Stan), to the King's former private hunting island (Kungliga Djurgården; now a city national park), there is plenty to keep you occupied. There's also a theme park, right in the heart of the city, as well a countless swimmable waterways and an entire archipelago to explore. Below are a few suggestions of what Stockholm has to offer.

The Vasa Museum

The Vasa Museum houses the eponymous ship, whose story is quite entertaining. In brief: in the 1700s the King of Sweden wanted a huge, 64 gun warship. His engineers set to work, but found the boat to be incredibly unstable. Nevertheless, the King demanded it be launched and, during its maiden voyage it travelled a short distance out the docks, before a light cross-breeze caused it capsize and sink in front of a crowd of thousands of Stockholmers.

The ABBA Museum

To an outside eye, ABBA is one of the few things that Sweden has going for it. While Swedish culture certainly does not begin and end with ABBA, the museum is a glitzy, camp tribute to the Swedish supertroupers, with plenty of interactive elements to keep all ages entertained.

Fotografiska

Fotografiska is a centre for contemporary photography and the visual arts. There are typically a number of exhibitions running at any given time, whose contents range from cultural commentary to dreamy surrealism. It is an eclectic mix, but well worth a visit.
